Overview

The cognitive architecture for Bob's Discount Furniture AI agent is designed to enhance the customer experience by structuring interactions around a well-defined hierarchy. The care group comprises two primary teams—Sales and Service—supported by an AI agent that serves as a concierge and automation assistant. The AI is empowered to address user inquiries, streamline processes, and ensure seamless interactions across various customer journeys. This ontology integrates the primary groups, user stages, topics, guides, tools, and measures to create a unified, efficient, and adaptive system.

Primary Groups

The AI first classifies inquiries into one of two primary groups: Sales or Service. Sales inquiries encompass requests for assistance with purchasing new furniture, understanding promotions, and customizing orders. Service inquiries cover post-purchase needs such as delivery status, rescheduling, warranty claims, or product issues. By distinguishing between these two groups, the AI ensures that the inquiry is routed to the appropriate team or handled autonomously where applicable. This differentiation allows the system to address user needs more effectively while aligning with the organizational structure.

Journey Stages

The AI further refines its understanding of the inquiry by identifying the user's stage in the Customer Journey. These stages include:

  • Pre-Purchase: Customers exploring product details, availability, or promotions.
  • Pre-Delivery Scheduling: Customers who have purchased and need to arrange delivery.
  • Delivery Scheduled: Customers confirming or preparing for their scheduled delivery.
  • Day of Delivery: Real-time coordination and tracking on the delivery day.
  • Recently Delivered: Assistance with immediate post-delivery needs such as assembly or damages.
  • Furniture in the Home: Long-term inquiries about care, warranty, or recycling.

Understanding the user stage enables the AI to provide tailored responses and proactively address potential concerns.

Topics

The AI classifies inquiries into well-differentiated Topics to streamline responses and guide users effectively. Key topics include: - Product Information Requests: Specifications, dimensions, and materials. - Order Placement Assistance: Support with purchasing furniture. - Delivery Scheduling: Coordination of delivery dates and times. - Order Modifications: Changes to color, size, or quantity. - Return and Exchange Inquiries: Policies and processes for returns. - Warranty Service Requests: Repairs or replacements under warranty. - Payment and Billing Issues: Financing, payment errors, and disputes. - Customer Feedback and Complaints: Logging feedback or addressing complaints. These topics allow the AI to quickly categorize and address customer inquiries, providing a structured approach to problem-solving.

Testing Scenarios for Bob's AI Agent

Scenario: Inquiry about Order Details

  • User Story: As a customer, I want to check the details of my order so that I can track its status.
  • Context: Customer has placed an order and wants to know the current status.
  • Initial User Input: I want to check the status of my order #123456.
  • Potential Escalation Scenarios: If the order status is unclear or there are issues with the order, escalate to customer support.
  • Expected Resolution: Provide the customer with the current status of their order, including delivery updates if applicable.
  • Acceptance Criteria: The customer receives accurate and detailed order status information.

Scenario: Schedule a Delivery

  • User Story: As a customer, I want to schedule my furniture delivery so that I can plan my day.
  • Context: Customer needs to select a delivery date and time window.
  • Initial User Input: I would like to schedule my delivery for next week.
  • Potential Escalation Scenarios: If the preferred date or time is unavailable, escalate to a delivery specialist.
  • Expected Resolution: Customer successfully schedules their delivery with confirmation of the date and time window.
  • Acceptance Criteria: Customer receives confirmation of their scheduled delivery, including any special instructions.

Scenario: Special Delivery Instructions

  • User Story: As a customer, I want to provide special instructions for my delivery so that it goes smoothly.
  • Context: Customer needs to communicate specific requirements for their delivery.
  • Initial User Input: Please ensure delivery is made through the back entrance.
  • Potential Escalation Scenarios: If the special request cannot be accommodated, escalate to a delivery specialist.
  • Expected Resolution: Special instructions are added to the delivery details, and the customer receives confirmation.
  • Acceptance Criteria: Customer successfully provides special instructions, and they are acknowledged and confirmed.
